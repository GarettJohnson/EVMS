Study Goals

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) has shown promising results in treating cognitive, emotional and behavioral disorders. In this study the subjects are undergoing treatment for Major Depressive Disorder and PTSD. Our goal is through the analysis of patient EEG signals to identify factors that can improve or hinder the effectiveness of TMS treatment.

The patients of this study received TMS treatment 5 times a week over the course of a period that ranged from 1-3 months. EEG signals were collected 4 times during treatment: once at the beginning, once at the end, and twice throughout the treatment.
